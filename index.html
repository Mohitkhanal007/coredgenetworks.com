CorEdge Networks

CorEdge Networks was a leading provider of high-performance networking hardware and low-latency Ethernet switching solutions for data centers, telecommunications, and high-performance computing (HPC) environments.

🔌 Core Technologies: Low-latency Ethernet switches · ATCA/MicroTCA platforms · 10GbE/20GbE solutions · Advanced Telecommunications Computing Architecture · High-performance computing infrastructure

Company Overview

CorEdge Networks specialized in developing cutting-edge networking solutions for demanding applications. The company's products were designed to meet the needs of telecommunications carriers, data center operators, and high-performance computing facilities requiring ultra-low latency and high bandwidth.

Key Products & Technologies

Low-Latency Ethernet Switches

CorEdge Networks demonstrated industry-leading low-latency Ethernet switching technology at major industry events including the Hot Interconnects Symposium at Stanford University. Their switches achieved sub-3.5 microsecond latency, setting new standards for performance.

ATCA & MicroTCA Solutions

The company developed Advanced Telecommunications Computing Architecture (ATCA) and MicroTCA platforms that provided flexible, reliable, and cost-effective solutions for telecommunications infrastructure. Their Virtual Carrier Manager (VCM) technology was among the industry's first working implementations.

OCTEON Multicore Processors

CorEdge Networks partnered with Cavium Networks to integrate OCTEON multicore processors into their 20Gbps Advanced Mezzanine Card (AMC) solutions, enabling advanced packet processing capabilities.

Industry Recognition

CorEdge Networks was featured in major technology publications including EDN, Design & Reuse, and LinuxHPC. The company collaborated with industry leaders such as Xilinx, Cavium, and Yamaichi to deliver innovative networking solutions.

Applications

Legacy

CorEdge Networks contributed significantly to the advancement of low-latency networking technology. The company's innovations in ATCA/MicroTCA platforms and high-performance Ethernet switching continue to influence modern networking infrastructure design.
